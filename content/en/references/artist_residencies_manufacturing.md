# Artist Residencies in Manufacturing & Industry

> A global guide to programs where artists work **inside active factories, foundries, and manufactories** alongside in-house craftspeople and industrial expertise.

---

## What Makes These Residencies Special

This is a rare category. Unlike converted industrial spaces repurposed as art centers, the programs below place artists **inside live production environments** — working alongside workers, using industrial equipment and materials, and collaborating directly with master craftspeople. The goal is mutual: the artist gains access to industrial knowledge and scale; the manufactory gains creative provocation and new perspectives.

---

## Tier 1 — Inside a Working Factory (The Pearls)

### Foundry & Metal Casting

---

### [Arts/Industry — John Michael Kohler Arts Center](https://www.jmkac.org/arts-industry/)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Location** | Kohler, Wisconsin, USA |
| **Material** | Cast iron, brass, vitreous china (ceramics) |
| **Duration** | 3 months (three cohorts per year) |
| **Deadline** | Opens January 1 each year — closes February 28 |
| **Access** | Open call |

The gold standard worldwide. Studios are located **directly on the factory production floor** of the Kohler Co. Pottery and Foundry — a working blue-collar manufacturing plant making plumbing fixtures. Artists work alongside union factory associates who have deep technical expertise in casting, molding, and firing.

**What you get:**
- 24/7 studio access on the factory floor
- Free industrial materials (casting metal, molding sand, vitreous clay, scrap wood/steel)
- Use of all factory equipment with technical assistance
- Housing + round-trip transportation
- Monthly honorarium + photographic services

**Structure:** 4 artists per cohort — 2 in Pottery, 2 in Foundry. No prior experience required.
**Notable alumni:** Theaster Gates, Robert Gober, Arlene Shechet, Ghada Amer, Willie Cole

### Ceramics & Porcelain — Inside Active Manufactories

---

### [Höchster Porzellan-Manufaktur (HPM) — "Exploring Porcelain"](https://www.hfg-offenbach.de/en/pages/hoechster-porzellan-manufaktur-hpm)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Location** | Frankfurt-Höchst, Germany |
| **Material** | Porcelain (Germany's 2nd oldest manufactory, founded 1746) |
| **Duration** | 4 months |
| **Deadline** | Next call expected May–June 2026 (for Oct 2026 start) |
| **Access** | Open call — runs twice per year |

Currently the most accessible factory-embedded porcelain residency in the world. Run in partnership with [HfG Offenbach](https://www.hfg-offenbach.de), artists work 4 days/week, 6–8 hours/day inside the production floor of a working 18th-century manufactory.

**What you get:**
- Studio workspace at the HPM + residency apartment in Offenbach
- €1,000/month stipend + travel costs (up to €1,500)
- Access to mold making, foundry, embossing, painting workshops
- Gas kiln (2.3m³, up to 1400°C) + multiple electric kilns
- New digital processes: ceramic 3D printing, robotics, digital surface finishing
- Documentation support + HfG academic network

**Requirements:** Postgraduate alumni with strong ceramics experience. Open internationally.
**Contact:** Nadya Bascha — [bascha@hfg-offenbach.de](mailto:bascha@hfg-offenbach.de)

---

### [Manufacture Nationale de Sèvres](https://www.sevresciteceramique.fr/en/)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Location** | Sèvres, France (near Paris) |
| **Material** | Porcelain (operating since the 18th century, French Ministry of Culture) |
| **Duration** | Variable (past residencies: 2–4 years) |
| **Access** | By invitation / direct proposal |

One of the most prestigious porcelain institutions in the world. Artists work alongside master craftspeople using techniques unchanged since the 18th century. Residencies involve deep collaboration with in-house artisans to develop ambitious porcelain projects impossible to realize anywhere else.

**Path in:** Operates primarily through institutional partnerships (e.g. the [Marcel Duchamp Prize / ADIAF](https://www.adiaf.com)). A well-crafted direct project proposal to their cultural team is a viable approach for artists with a strong practice.

---

### [Meissen Porzellan Manufaktur — artCAMPUS](https://www.meissen.com/net/atelier)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Location** | Meissen, Germany (near Leipzig) |
| **Material** | Porcelain (Europe's oldest manufactory, founded 1710) |
| **Duration** | Variable (2 weeks to several months) |
| **Access** | By invitation / direct proposal |

Since 2010, Meissen has run an artist-in-residence program giving artists access to its facilities and master artisans. Artists receive great freedom but also a great challenge: porcelain here is worked at an extraordinary level of precision. Residents have come from Syria, Tunisia, India, South Korea, the USA, Switzerland, and Germany.

**What you get:**
- Access to master artisans with centuries of collective expertise
- Use of historic mold library and all production facilities
- Freedom to work in utilitarian ware or pure art — or both

**Path in:** Contact their artistic direction with a project proposal. Artists are typically invited following an interview and factory tour.

### [Wedgwood Artist in Residence](https://www.wedgwood.com)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Location** | Barlaston, Stoke-on-Trent, UK |
| **Material** | Ceramics / china (Jasperware, bone china — founded 1759) |
| **Duration** | Variable |
| **Access** | By invitation / direct proposal |

Wedgwood explicitly invites creatives to **work alongside the craftspeople within their factory**, expressing creative vision through manufacturing techniques and ceramic materials. The collaboration with factory workers is at the heart of the program.

**Path in:** A compelling project concept that engages with Wedgwood's history, techniques, and archive is the key — direct approach to their creative/cultural direction.

### Textile — Inside Active Textile Industry

---

### [TaDA — Textile and Design Alliance](https://tada-residency.ch/en/residence)

| | |
|---|---|
| **Location** | Arbon, Eastern Switzerland |
| **Material** | Textile — all disciplines |
| **Duration** | 3 months (April–June or September–November) |
| **Deadline** | **August 12, 2026** — 2027 call OPEN NOW |
| **Access** | Open call |

The closest equivalent to Kohler Arts/Industry for textiles. Artists work in a studio inside the **former Saurer AG textile and engine factory** and are connected directly with 12 active textile companies in Eastern Switzerland. The exchange is mutual: residents bring creative provocation; companies bring industrial expertise and production scale.

**What you get:**
- Private room in shared apartment in Arbon, near Lake Constance
- Workspace in Kreativzentrum ZIK (former Saurer AG factory)
- CHF 2,000/month living expense contribution
- Travel costs covered
- Professional exchange with 12 partner textile companies + access to their infrastructure
- Individual mentoring by textile designer Martin Leuthold and external experts
- Public presentation at "TaDA Spinnerei" event

**Open to:** Any discipline, any nationality. Must have demonstrated interest in textile culture, sustainability, and production.

## Tier 2 — Art Centers with Strong Industrial Workshop Access

These programs don't embed artists inside an operating commercial factory, but offer serious access to industrial-grade materials and processes, often in post-industrial settings with technical support staff.

---

| | |
|---|---|
| **Location** | Genk, Belgium (C-mine site, former coal mine) |
| **Materials** | Wood, metal, ceramics, digital |
| **Duration** | 4 months |
| **Access** | Open call — annual, typically published in fall |

Based in a post-industrial landscape and explicitly connected to Genk's industrial heritage. Artists have access to well-equipped workshops for wood, metal, ceramics, and digital production, plus possible use of [LUCA C-mine](https://www.c-mine.be) art college facilities.

**What you get:**

- €3,000 honorarium (individual) / €5,000 (duo) / €7,000 (collective)

- Coaching, studio visits by cultural professionals
- Free admission to all museums in Flanders and Brussels

## How to Approach the Invitation-Only Programs

For **Sèvres**, **Meissen**, and **Wedgwood** — which don't have open calls — the path in is a direct, well-crafted project proposal:

1. Research their specific materials, historical archive, and recent artist collaborations
2. Craft a project proposal that speaks to what their craftspeople can offer that no other place in the world can
3. Contact their **artistic/cultural direction directly** — not the commercial team
4. A studio visit or factory tour is often the first step before a residency is formalized
