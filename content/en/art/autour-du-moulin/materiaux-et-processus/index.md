#### Reflection on materials
This research project consists of creating a model of a kinetic sculpture made from recycled materials that is energetically autonomous. The original idea proposes the creation of a single monumental element, but during this research and exploration phase I want to free myself from all preconceived ideas and remain open to the possibility of an installation composed of several complementary elements in interaction.

I work intuitively so that the material research phase puts me in contact with concrete reality. I let myself be guided by what arises while making concrete gestures of interaction with the world. Spontaneity always operates, but on a plane where the apparent opposition of the real world and imagination enters into synergy.
The primary functions of each material element are based on 3 main needs:
- structural
- technical
- aesthetic.

Each element must fulfil these three functions and serve to transform natural energy into movement (kinetic energy), to visualize this transformation and participate in the energy autonomy of the whole. This concept of autonomy does not necessarily imply that the work must be operational at all times. On the contrary, it supposes that the work operates with the available energy and, depending on conditions, can find itself in a state of apathy when there is no energy and/or in a state of euphoria when there is an excess. This brings us back to the question: what do we do when there is an energy outage and what do we do when there is too much. What circularity means is at the centre of the debate and is also addressed through the provenance of the materials. Both their symbolic quality and their origin essentially consist of restoring their value to reveal the knowledge they materialize; often characterized by their original function and henceforth by their recontextualization.

The objective is to integrate as many used or recycled elements as possible. This can become very broad which represents an intrinsic risk to the research and exploration I am undertaking with this project. What proportion of the materials forming the sculpture can come from used sources? And does this affect the integrity of the work and its fundamental value?

#### Making contact
Jérome Bouchard from FabBulles referred me to Ms. Emilie Dupont of [La Société d'Aide aux développement des Communauté du Kamouraska - SADC](https://www.sadc-cae.ca/fr/sadc/sadc-kamouraska/) who referred me to Alexandre Jolicoeur, coordinator of the Industrial Symbiosis project, through which I was able to obtain a listing of all industries in the region with surplus materials. The list is very comprehensive but not very practical or reliable. That said, during a conversation with Alexandre we were able to discuss the project and he suggested a few contacts working in areas of interest for the project.

- D'Amour métal, Lislet.

With Alexandre Jolicoeur we took the opportunity to discuss possible approaches to visualizing natural energy and its accumulation:

- solar phototropism (parabola coated with reflective material) as an alternative to photovoltaics based on the concept of liquid or solid expansion.
- Peltier element for very low voltage signal using as an example a bi-material plate (copper and steel)
- For energy storage or better said the 'battery effect' we talked about water columns and the pumping principle, citing the old manual pump once used to extract water from wells.

I developed the ritual of religiously visiting the eco centre — that place where objects people come to throw away are sorted. For the prototyping phase this suited me perfectly and I found some very interesting elements.

A friend also gave me old tripods which I appreciated as they are very useful for supporting elements and being able to easily adjust angles, height and position in space. One of those she gave me is a remarkable object; very tall, solid and of great quality. I immediately took the opportunity to hang a bicycle wheel from it which will serve the part of my research on optical effects through kinetics.
